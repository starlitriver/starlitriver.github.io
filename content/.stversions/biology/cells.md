this page is intended to give an overview about cells in biology, and in-depth explanations will be provided as links to other pages - this means that there won't be much details in this page

first and foremost, it is recommended to look at [wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia-on-ipfs.org/wiki/Cell_(biology)?useskin=vector) which is very detailed; i will give a summary here:

* cells are the smallest structural and functional unit of an organism

* all cells consists of [cytoplasm](https://en.wikipedia-on-ipfs.org/wiki/Cytoplasm "Cytoplasm") enclosed within a [membrane](https://en.wikipedia-on-ipfs.org/wiki/Cell_membrane "Cell membrane"), and contains many [macromolecules](https://en.wikipedia-on-ipfs.org/wiki/Macromolecule "Macromolecule") such as [proteins](https://en.wikipedia-on-ipfs.org/wiki/Protein "Protein"), [DNA](https://en.wikipedia-on-ipfs.org/wiki/DNA "DNA") and [RNA](https://en.wikipedia-on-ipfs.org/wiki/RNA "RNA"), as well as many [small molecules](https://en.wikipedia-on-ipfs.org/wiki/Small_molecule "Small molecule") of nutrients and [metabolites](https://en.wikipedia-on-ipfs.org/wiki/Metabolite "Metabolite")

* different cells have different [[cell sizes]] but most plant and animal cells have dimensions between 1 and 100 [micrometres](https://en.wikipedia-on-ipfs.org/wiki/Micrometre "Micrometre")

* there are [prokaryotes](https://en.wikipedia-on-ipfs.org/wiki/Prokaryote) and [eucaryotes](https://en.wikipedia-on-ipfs.org/wiki/Eukaryote); see the [[differences between prokaryotes and eukaryotes]]

the [[cell membrane]] is an effective barrier to seperate the [[intracellular fluid]] and the [[extracellular fluid]]
