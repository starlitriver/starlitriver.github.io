blood is a body fluid in the [[circulatory system]] that delivers necessary substances such as nutrients and oxygen to the [[cell|cells]] and transports [[metabolic waste]] away from cells

blood performs many important functions within the body
- supply of oxygen to tissues which is bound to [[hemoglobin]]
- Supply of nutrients such as glucose, amino acids, and 
- Removal of waste such as carbon dioxide, urea, and lactic acid
- Immunological functions, including circulation of white blood cells, and detection of foreign material by antibodies
- Coagulation, the response to a broken blood vessel, the conversion of blood from a liquid to a semisolid gel to stop bleeding
- Messenger functions, including the transport of hormones and the signaling of tissue damage
- Regulation of core body temperature
- Hydraulic functions
