hemoglobin is a [[protein]] that facilitates the transport of oxygen in [[red blood cell|red blood cells]]

in mammals, hemoglobin makes up 96% of a red blood cell's dry weight and around 35% of the total weight

hemoglobin has an oxygen-binding capacity of 1.34 ml of $\ce{O2}$ per gram

the mammalian hemoglobin molecule can bind to up to 4 oxygen molecules

hemoglobin is also able to transport other gases
it carries off about 25% of the carbon dioxide in the respiratory system
