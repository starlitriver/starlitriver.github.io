the kidneys are for
- maintain good amount of water
- maintain good levels of chemicals

on the average day a kidney filters about 180 liters of blood, but only about 1.5 liters end up as urine.
kidneys have many nephrons - they are used to filter liquid and reabsorb some contents that needed to be kept.

the blood from the [[heart]] enters the kidneys through renal arteries, which has a system of capillaries until it enters a tangle of porous capillaries called the glomerulus
this is the starting point for a single nephron
the pressure in the glomerulus is high enough that it squeezes about 20% of the fluid out of the blood into a cup shaped sack called the Bowman's capsule.
the stuff that got squeezed out is now called filtrate. it is made out of water, urea, and smaller ions and molecules like sodium, glucose and amino acids.
on the other hand, the red blood cells and the proteins do not get filtered.
from the Bowman's capsule, it flows into the loop of Henle. it extracts most of the water that we need from the filtrate and other things that get reabsorbed back into the blood.
