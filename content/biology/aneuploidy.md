in biology, aneuploidy refers to the condition where the number of chromosomes, the diploid number, of an organism is abnormal

aneuploidy can happen in allosomes and autosomes

aneuploidy in human allosomes may result in the following disorders:
XO - Turner symdrome
XXX - Metafemale
XXXX - Metafemale, with symptoms of Down symdrome
XYY - Jacob syndrome
XXY - Klinefelter syndrome
XXXY - Klinefelter syndrome, severe

depending on the number of allosomes a person has, the aneuploidy is classified as nullisomy, monosomy, trisomy, or polysomy
