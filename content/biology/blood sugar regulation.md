in biology, blood sugar regulation is the process by which the levels of blood sugar are maintained in the body within a narrow range

blood sugar regulation, also called glucose homeostasis, is one aspect of [[homeostasis]]

insulin, which lowers blood sugar, is excreted by beta ($\beta$) cells of the pancreas
glucagon, which raises blood sugar, is excreted by alpha ($\alpha$) cells of the pancreas

some cells store glucose in the form of glycogen, which can be broken down to quickly release glucose into the blood
