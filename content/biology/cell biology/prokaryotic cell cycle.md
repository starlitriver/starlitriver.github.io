All prokaryotic cells replicate using binary fission.


There are 2 types of prokaryotic cells, bacteria and archaea.

Prokaryotic cells do not contain a nucleus or membrane bound organelles.

The cell wall of most bacteria is made of peptidoglycan.

They have one circular chromosome of DNA which floats in the cytoplasm, called the nucleoid, as well as small circular pieces of dna called plasmids.


Binary Fission

Cellular contents and DNA are replicated into 2, the product of  this type of replication is 2 identical daughter cells; binary fission uses less energy and is faster, compared to other types of replication.

1. The cell replicates its DNA from the nucleoid and plasmids.
    
2. The cell continues to elongate, and the replicated DNA migrates to opposite poles of the cell.
    
3. In the equator of the cell, a z-ring pulls the cell wall and membrane from both sides, creating a septum where the cell is dividing.
    
4. The z-ring contracts until the 2 daughter cells are separated.
    
5. In the end, there are 2 genetically identical daughter cells.
