the endocrine system is responsible for producing hormones (signalling molecules) that affect bodily functions.

a signalling molecule can
- a molecule that can interact with and initiate a response in a target cell
- instruct cells to do a variety of things such as alter gene expression
- open and close protein channels
- release other signalling molecules, or even die

the endocrine system collaborates with the nervous system to react to environmental changes, fluctuating hormonal changes that affect many functions in the body.
