Regulation of temperature


Homeothermy is the maintainable of body temperature within narrow limits.

The hypothalamus measures your body temperature.

If too hot, stimulates sweat glands, and the vasodilation of arterioles.

If too cold, your skeletal muscles shiver and produce heat. Vasoconstriction of blood vessels and contraction of the arrector pili.

Behavioural responses are voluntary.

Structural adaptations are physical constructions of an organism that help homestasis. For example, fat and fur helps to stay warm.


Poikiloterms don't have physiological mechanisms to maintain homeostasis temperature. Rather, they allow their body temperature to fluctuate and use behaviours to voluntarily change the temperature.



Regulation of blood glucose


Glucose causes osmosis, so it cannot be simply be stored in water.

Animals stores glucose as an insoluble polymer made of glucose, called glycogen.

The islets of Langerhans are endocrine tissue in the pancreas that measure blood glucose concentration and release two hormones, insulin from beta cells, and glucagon from alpha cells.

Insulin stimulates liver, skeletal muscle and other cells to take glucose from the blood and store it as glycogen.
Glucagon only stimulates the liver to release more glucose.

Both the alpha and beta cells detect the blood sugar levels, and the cells produce less of one hormone and more of another to regulate.


Regulation of water balance

The amount of water in your urine can e regulated.

ADH is release from the posterior pituitary in respoonse to high blood tonicity. It targets the collecting tubules in the nephron, where it stimulates insertion of aquaporine into the membrane. This increases the amount of water reabsorption in the nephron.
