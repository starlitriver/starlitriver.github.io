in biology, the immune system is a network of biological systems that protects an organism from diseases

it detects and responds to a wide variety of pathogens and other things that distinguish from the organism's own healthy tissue

many species have two major subsystems of the immune system: the [[innate immune system]] and the [[adaptive immune system]]
the innate immune system provides a preconfigured response to broad groups of situations while the adaptive immune system provides a tailored response to each situation by learning to recognize molecules it has previously encountered
