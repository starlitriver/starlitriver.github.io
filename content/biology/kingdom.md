In biological [[taxonomy]], kingdoms are a classification group below [[domain]] and above phylum.

There are 6 kingdoms; archaea, bacteria, protista, fungi, plantae and animalia.

Does this seem weird because it doesn't match any models? Let's complain to the Victorian curriculum. Note that this is for convenience, and there are many different models, see [wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_(biology)#Summary).
