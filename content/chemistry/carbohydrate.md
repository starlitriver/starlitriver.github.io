in chemistry, a carbohydrate is defined as a [[biomolecule|biomolecule]] that consists of carbon, hydrogen and oxygen atoms, usually with a hydrogen–oxygen ratio of 2:1

a carbohydrate consists of one or more [[monosaccharide|monosaccharides]]

a carbohydrate is either classified as a monosaccharide, disaccharide, oligosaccharide or polysaccharide
if a carbohydrate contains 2 monosaccharide units it's called a disaccharide; if 3 to 10 units it's called a oligosaccharide; if more than 10 it's called a polysaccharide

[[polysaccharide|polysaccharides]] are the most abundant carbohydrates
