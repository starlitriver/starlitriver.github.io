in chemistry, a fatty acid is a carboxylic acid with an aliphatic chain which is either saturated or unsaturated

a fatty acid consists of a straight chain of an even number of carbon atoms, with a carboxyl group $\text{COOH}$ at one end and a methyl group $\text{CH}_3$ at another

saturated fatty acids have no C=C double bonds

unsaturated fatty acids have one or more C=C double bonds
