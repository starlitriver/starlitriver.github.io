also called a cubic equation (the graph of which is called a cubic graph so there isn't another fancy word to haunt you)

a cubic equation has an inflection point, which is the point at which the gradient changes sign (negative or positive)
#### standard form
$f(x)=ax^3+bx^2+cx+d$
where a b c d are constants
#### vertex form
$f(x)=a(x-h)^3+k$
where a h k are constants and (h,k) is the inflection point of the equation
#### product form
$𝑓(𝑥) = 𝑎(𝑥 − 𝑥_1)(𝑥 − 𝑥_2)(𝑥 − 𝑥_3)$
