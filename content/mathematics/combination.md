in mathematics, a combination is a selection of items from a set that has distinct members, such that the order of selection does not matter (unlike [[permutation|permutations]])


a k-combination of a set $S$ is a subset of $k$ that has $n$ distinct elements of $S$
k-combinations are represented with $C(n,k)={}^{n}C_{k}=C_{k}^{n}$
if the set has $n$ elements, the number of k-combinations is $C(n,k)=\dfrac{n!}{k!(n-k)!}$
