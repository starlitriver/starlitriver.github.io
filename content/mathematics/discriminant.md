in algebra, the discriminant of a [[polynomial]] is a quantity that depends on the coefficients and allows deducing some properties of the roots
the discriminant is represented with $\Delta$

## 2 degree polynomial

the discriminant for a [[2 degree polynomial]] in the form $ax^2+bx+c$ is $\Delta=b^2-4ac$
if discriminant $>0$ then there are 2 real solutions
if discriminant $=0$ then there is 1 real solution
if discriminant $<0$ then there are no real solutions
