## completing the square

we can try to factor things by manipulating the expression so that we can use the perfect square law

### $x^2+6x+5=0$

complete the square
$x^2+6x+9-9+5=0$
$x^2+6x+9-4=0$
$(x+3)^2-4=0$

>solve
>$(x+3)^2=4$
>$x+3=\pm 2$
>$x=\pm 2-3$
>$x=-5,x=1$
>
>difference of squares
>$(x+3)^2-(2)^2=0$
>$(x+3+2)(x+3-2)=0$
>$(x+5)(x+1)=0$
>$x+5=0,x-1=0$
>$x=-5,x=1$

### $x^2+6x-41=0$

complete the square
$x^2+6x+9-9-41=0$
$x^2+6x+9-50=0$
$(x+3)^2-50=0$

>solve
>$(x+3)^2=50$
>$x+3=\pm \sqrt{50}$
>$x=\pm 5\sqrt{2} -3$
>$x=5\sqrt{2} -3,x=-5\sqrt{2} -3$

### $x^2+4x+5=0$

complete the square
$x^2+4x+4+-4+5=0$
$x^2+4x+4+1=0$
$(x+2)^2+1=0$

>solve
>$(x+2)^2=-1$
>$x=\pm \sqrt{-1}-2$
>this solution is not a real number. it is a complex number.
