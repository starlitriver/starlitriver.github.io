a polynomial is a math expression that has variables and only the operations addition, multiplication and exponentiation to positive integer powers, and has a finite number of terms

## terms
a polynomial with one term is a [[monomial]]
a polynomial with two terms is a [[binomial]]
a polynomial with three terms is a [[trinomial]]
thankfully polynomials with more than three terms don't have special names

## degree
a polynomial's highest [[exponentiation|exponent]] is called the degree of the polynomial
- [[2 degree polynomial]]
- [[3 degree polynomial]]
- [[4 degree polynomial]]

## graphical features
### y-intercept
to find the y-intercept of a polynomial $f(x)$, we just need to make $x=0$ and find $f(0)$

### x-intercepts
the x-intercepts of a polynomial is also called the roots of the polynomial

to find the x-intercepts (zeros) of a polynomial $f(x)$, we need to find all $x$ values for which $f(x)=0$
the most common way to do this is to factorize and use the zero-product property

s a quantity that depends on the coefficients and allows deducing some properties of the roots
