a polynomial is a math expression that has variables and only the operations addition, multiplication and exponentiation to positive integer powers, and has a finite number of terms

a polynomial's highest exponent is called the degree of the polynomial

you can [[determine polynomial using points|determine the equation of a polynomial with known points]]

we deal with polynomials of different degrees differently
- [[2 degree polynomial]]
- [[3 degree polynomial]]
- [[4 degree polynomial]]

## y-intercept
to find the y-intercept of a polynomial $f(x)$, we just need to make $x=0$ and find $f(0)$

## x-intercepts
to find the x-intercepts (zeros) of a polynomial $f(x)$, we need to find all $x$ values for which $f(x)=0$
the most common way to do this is to factorize and use the zero-product property
