stem cells can undergo [[cellular differentiation]]

What are stem cells?

Two properties of stem cells: Self-renewal and Potency:

Self Renewal - This means a stem cell can produce both a differentiated cell and a copy of itself.

Potency - Measures its capacity to differentiate into different cell types.  
Different types of stem cells - 

- Different types of stem cells occur in your body and they can be identified in terms of their ability to produce different cell types.
    

Main types of stem cells - 

- Totipotent - Stem cells that can differentiate into any type.
    
- Pluripotent - Stem cells that can differentiate into multiple cell types.
    
- Multipotent - Stem cells that can differentiate into a limited number of specialised cell types belonging to a specific tissue or organ.
    
- Unipotent - These stem cells can only produce only one cell type but have the property of self-renewal that distinguishes them from non-stem cells.
    

  

Summary:

- Stem cells are cells that are not fully differentiated. They have the potential to divide indefinitely to produce daughter cells, which can differentiate into various cells.
    
- Potency of a stem cell describes the extent of its ability to differentiate into different cell types.
