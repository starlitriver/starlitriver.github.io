in biology, the extracellular matrix (ECM), also called the intercellular matrix (ICM), is a network consisting of extracellular macromolecules and minerals that provide structural and chemical support to surrounding cells

the extracellular matrix is composed of
- collagen
- enzymes
- glycoproteins
- hydroxyapatite

in animals
- collagen fibers and bone mineral comprise the ECM of bone tissue
- reticular fibers and ground substance comprise the ECM of loose connective tissue
- blood plasma is the ECM of blood

in plants
- the ECM includes cell wall components like cellulose
