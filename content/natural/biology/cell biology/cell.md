in biology, a cell is the smallest structural and functional unit of an [[organism]]

cells are between organelles and tissues in [[biological organization]]

the two main types of cells are [[prokaryote|prokaryotes]] and [[eukaryote|eukaryotes]]

everything inside the cell except the [[nucleus]] is called the [[cytoplasm]], enclosed within the [[cell membrane]] and held together by the [[cytoskeleton]]


all cells have [[organelle|organelles]], the "organs" of the cells, which are essential for cell functions

because organelles bring molecules together to react and achieve a purpose, without organelles these reactions are harder - if these reactions do not happen quickly enough, the cell dies

## cell size

the surface area of a cell determines how many molecules can move in and out of the cell
therefore, cells need to optimize their [[surface area to volume ratio]] - bigger cells need more energy and produce more waste, so cells that are too large cannot survive

therefore, the cells with organelles are always larger than cells without because the ones without are not large enough to need organelles.
because of surface area to volume ratio and organelles, cells have difference sizes
