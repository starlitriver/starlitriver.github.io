in biology, the innate immune system is a part of the [[immune system]] that provides a preconfigured response to broad groups of situations

for the innate immune system to work, it needs to have a mechanism by which it can identify pathogens
cells of the immune system have receptors that detect what is not part of the organism

in humans, these are the cells in the innate immune system


| cell                | description                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         |
| ------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| macrophage          | macrophage means big eater in greek. these cells ingest pathogens, particularly bacteria. once the pathogens have been gobbled up, they are destroyed inside the macrophage. cells that eat things are called phagocytes, meaning eating cell in greek. macrophages are phagocytes. |
| neutrophil          | neutrophils are the most abundant cells in the immune system. they do a few things, but just like macrophages, they are phagocytes. unfortunately, neutrophils are a little worse at eating and will die once they have ingested a pathogen.                                        |
| mast cell           | mast cells respond to certain kinds of pathogens (e.g. worms). they are perhaps more important in their role in creating an allergic response. they release a chemical called histamine.                                                                                            |
| eosinophil          | eosinophils are similar to mast cells. very little is known about them and they are unlikely to feature prominently in your vce studies, but if you see this term, just know that they are white blood cells that fight disease.                                                    |
| natural killer cell | perhaps the best-named cell in the entire body, natural killer cells help to clear viral infections. viruses tend to end up inside cells, rather than living outside of them like bacteria. natural killer cells help virally infected cells die so that viruses die too.           |
| dendritic cell      | dendritic cells are an important bridge between the innate and adaptive immune systems. they gobble up heaps of stuff from the environment around them and present (i.e. show) it on their surface in mhc class ii molecules                                                        |
